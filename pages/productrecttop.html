The 2016 1 oz Silver Maple Leaf features a new form of security though the Royal Canadian Mint’s Bullion DNA program. The Bullion DNA program offers the highest security on the market for any bullion coin. Each bullion coin features a textured maple leaf security privy mark, micro-engraved with laser technology and is visible only under magnification. Etched within the maple leaf security mark are the two digits indicating the coin’s production year. Each micro-engraving is encoded, scanned and recorded at the mint for future authentication giving each coin their unique Bullion DNA.

Bullion DNA authenticates coins by scanning them with a Mint’s proprietary Bullion DNA reader verifying that the micro-engraved security mark is a match to the Royal Canadian Mint’s secure database. This process provides each Silver Maple Leaf coin with the highest security for any coin in the market place. The Royal Canadian Mint’s unique Bullion DNA program authenticates Silver Maple Leaf coins dated 2015 and later. APMEX is a recognized Bullion DNA dealer for trusted RCM products.

History

The history of Canadian Maple Leaf coins

The beautiful 2016 1 oz Silver Maple Leaf is part of the proud history of the Royal Canadian Mint. The Gold Canadian Maple Leaf was launched in 1979 as a three-year program and it was an immediate success. Silver coins with the Maple Leaf design were created in 1988 to follow up on the success of the Gold Maple Leaf. The mint has added other 1 oz Silver bullion coin series, but the maple leaf remains its most iconic design.

Why invest in 2016 1 oz Silver Maple Leaf coins?

The 2016 1 oz Silver Maple Leaf is a legal tender coin with a face value of $5, though it is worth much more based on the Silver spot price. Silver coins are also a popular choice for investors who want to hold a currency with an intrinsic value to hedge against a collapse of paper currencies. A 1 oz Silver bullion coin is often easier to buy or sell, adding flexibility to your investment management.

The worth of Silver coins is, in part, based on the many uses of Silver.

A 2016 1 oz Silver Maple leaf gets much of its value from Silver’s many industrial uses, starting with its use in photography in 1727. The metal in Silver coins is reasonably abundant in the earth, but its extraordinary ability to conduct heat and electricity, and its antimicrobial properties create a rising demand, outstripping the supply nearly every year since 1990. A Canadian Maple Leaf is more pure than most jewelry, another major use of Silver, because Silver is soft and needs to be alloyed with another metal for daily use. A 1 oz Silver bullion coin represents humanity’s enduring appreciation of Silver, both for its beauty and its usefulness.
